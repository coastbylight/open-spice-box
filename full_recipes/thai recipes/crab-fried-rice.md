# Thai Crab Fried Rice (ข้าวผัดปู)

*Jasmine rice wok-fried with crab, garlic, Thai chilies, egg, and fish sauce, served with cucumber, lime, and fresh cilantro*

---

## Headnote

Thai fried rice is simpler and lighter than its Chinese counterpart. Where Chinese fried rice builds layers of soy sauce, oyster sauce, and multiple proteins, Thai fried rice relies on fewer ingredients and lets each one speak clearly. This version uses crab, and the whole point is to taste the crab. Everything else, the garlic, the chilies, the fish sauce, the egg, supports the sweet, briny crab without competing with it.

The technique matters more than the ingredient list. The rice must be cooked and cooled, either freshly made and spread on a tray for five minutes, or day-old from the fridge. Wet, hot rice steams in the wok and turns to mush. Dry, cold rice fries properly, developing a lightly chewy texture with slightly crispy edges. The rice is cooked in two batches to avoid overcrowding the wok, which is the difference between fried rice and steamed rice that happens to be in a wok.

The egg is scrambled directly in the wok after pushing the rice aside, broken into small bits, and then tossed through. The crab goes in at the very end with the scallions and fish sauce, heated through but not cooked further. White pepper rather than black is the traditional Thai seasoning, and it goes on at the end along with a handful of minced cilantro. The plate comes to the table with cucumber slices, lime wedges, and extra fish sauce on the side, and the person eating it adjusts the seasoning to their taste. That is the Thai approach: the cook gets it close, the diner makes it perfect.

## Ingredients {#ingredients}

- 350 g cooked jasmine rice (about 2 cups), day-old or freshly cooked and cooled
- 1 1/2 tbsp vegetable oil, divided
- 1 to 3 Thai bird chilies, finely sliced (adjust to taste)
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 large egg
- Salt
- 115 g picked cooked crabmeat (4 oz)
- 2 scallions, thinly sliced
- 2 tsp fish sauce, plus more for serving
- Ground white pepper
- 8 g fresh cilantro leaves (1/4 cup), minced

### For serving
- 1 Japanese cucumber, thinly sliced
- 1 lime, cut into wedges
- Extra sliced Thai chilies
- Extra fish sauce

---

## Method

1. Fry the rice in batches. Heat 1/2 tablespoon of oil in a wok over high heat until lightly smoking. Add the chilies and garlic, cook for 10 seconds until fragrant. Add half the rice, stirring and tossing until pale brown with a lightly chewy texture, about 3 minutes. Transfer to a bowl. Repeat with another 1/2 tablespoon of oil and the remaining rice. Return the first batch and combine.

2. Scramble the egg. Push the rice to the side. Add the remaining 1/2 tablespoon of oil to the empty space. Crack the egg in, season with salt, and scramble into small bits. Toss with the rice.

3. Add the crab and finish. Add the crabmeat, scallions, and fish sauce. Toss constantly until the crab is heated through and the scallions soften, about 1 minute. Season with white pepper and salt. Stir in the cilantro.

4. Serve immediately with cucumber slices, lime wedges, extra fish sauce, and sliced chilies on the side.

---

## Why This Works

Cooking the rice in two batches prevents overcrowding the wok. Overcrowded rice steams instead of frying, producing a wet, clumpy result. Two batches ensure every grain gets contact with the hot metal and develops the lightly chewy, slightly crispy texture that defines good fried rice.

Jasmine rice is the traditional choice. Its floral aroma and slightly sticky texture are part of the Thai fried rice character. Chinese medium-grain or Japanese sushi rice work as alternatives but produce a different result.

The crab goes in last and is only heated through, not cooked further. Crab that is stir-fried too long toughens and loses its delicate sweetness.

## Ingredient & Health Notes

**Crab:** High in protein, low in fat, and one of the richest food sources of zinc and B12. The natural sweetness of crab is what makes this fried rice special.

**Fish sauce:** The primary seasoning in Thai fried rice, replacing the soy sauce used in Chinese versions. It provides salt and umami in a single ingredient.

---

## Substitutions and Variations

**Shrimp:** 225g of peeled shrimp can replace the crab. Add with the garlic and cook until pink before adding the rice.

**Chicken or pork:** Pre-cook 225g of sliced meat and add with the rice.

**Vegetarian:** Replace crab with diced firm tofu and fish sauce with soy sauce.

---

## Serving Suggestions

Thai fried rice is a complete meal. Serve with the traditional accompaniments: cucumber slices, lime wedges, and extra fish sauce. For a fuller spread, add [Larb Gai](/recipes/larb-gai) and a simple Thai soup.

---

## Storage and Reheating

**Refrigerator:** Store for up to 1 day. Reheat in a hot wok with a splash of water.

**Freezing:** Not recommended.
