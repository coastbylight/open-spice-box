# Shrimp and Broccoli in Brown Sauce (虾仁西兰花)

*Blanched shrimp and broccoli tossed in a savory brown sauce of soy, oyster sauce, and garlic, the Chinese takeout dish done right*

---

## Headnote

This was one of the most frequently ordered dishes at The Woks of Life family restaurant, and the reason is obvious: plump shrimp, bright broccoli, and a glossy brown sauce that makes you want to scrape the last drop from the plate with a spoon and steamed rice. It is comfort food that also happens to be light and fast.

The technique is the same blanch-then-sauce method used for chicken and broccoli, but applied to shrimp, which are even more unforgiving of overcooking. The broccoli gets 30 seconds in boiling water. The shrimp get 15 seconds, just enough to turn pink and curl into a C shape. They come out at about 60 to 70 percent done and finish cooking in the sauce, which means they end up tender and plump rather than rubbery. This blanching step also prevents the shrimp from releasing their liquid into the wok, which would thin the sauce and steam everything instead of frying it.

The brown sauce is darker and more savory than the white sauce version of this genre. Soy sauce and dark soy sauce provide salt and color. Oyster sauce adds sweetness and body. Chicken stock gives it volume. A cornstarch slurry thickens everything into a glaze that clings to the shrimp and broccoli without pooling. The whole dish comes together in the wok in about two minutes once the components are blanched, and it is on the table in the time it takes to order delivery.

## Ingredients {#ingredients}

- 340 g shrimp (12 oz), about 12 large (16-20 count), peeled, deveined, and butterflied
- 280 g broccoli florets (10 oz, about 3 cups)
- 120 ml low-sodium chicken stock (1/2 cup), warmed
- 1/4 tsp sugar
- 1 1/2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1/2 tsp dark soy sauce
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1/2 tsp sesame oil
- 1/8 tsp white pepper
- 2 tbsp canola or vegetable oil
- 2 cloves garlic, chopped
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine
- 1 1/2 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water (slurry)

---

## Method

1. Prepare the sauce. Mix the chicken stock, sugar, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, and white pepper. Set aside.

2. Blanch the broccoli. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Blanch the broccoli for 30 seconds. Remove and drain. Let the water return to a boil.

3. Blanch the shrimp. Add the shrimp to the boiling water for 15 seconds. They will be about 60 to 70 percent cooked, just turning pink. Remove and drain immediately.

4. Build the sauce. Heat the wok over high heat. Add the oil around the edge. Add the garlic and the Shaoxing wine. Add the sauce mixture. Bring to a boil.

5. Finish. Add the shrimp and broccoli back to the wok. Once everything comes to a boil, drizzle in the cornstarch slurry, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens and clings to the shrimp and broccoli. There should be no pooling sauce. Serve immediately with steamed white rice.

---

## Why This Works

Blanching the shrimp for only 15 seconds sets the exterior and prevents them from releasing liquid into the wok. Without blanching, shrimp dump water as they cook, which thins the sauce and turns the stir-fry into a steam bath. The 15-second blanch gets them 60 to 70 percent done. The remaining cooking happens in the sauce, which means the shrimp finish at exactly the right moment.

The brown sauce is a balance of four flavors: soy sauce for salt, dark soy sauce for color, oyster sauce for sweetness and umami, and chicken stock for body. The cornstarch slurry transforms this thin liquid into a concentrated glaze that clings rather than pools. The sauce should coat everything with nothing left on the plate.

## Ingredient & Health Notes

**Shrimp:** A lean protein high in selenium, B12, iodine, and omega-3 fatty acids. Buy frozen, shell-on shrimp for best quality. Flash-frozen shrimp are better than "fresh" shrimp that have been thawed and sitting on ice.

**Broccoli:** Brief blanching (30 seconds) preserves vitamin C and sulforaphane better than long cooking.

---

## Substitutions and Variations

**White sauce version:** Replace the soy sauces and oyster sauce with 1 tsp salt, 1/4 tsp sugar, 1/2 tsp sesame oil, and 1 cup chicken stock.

**Scallops:** Large sea scallops work with the same technique. Blanch 20 seconds instead of 15.

**Snow peas or snap peas:** Can replace or supplement the broccoli. Blanch 15 seconds.

**Extra sauce:** Some prefer 1.5x the sauce for extra over rice.

## Storage and Reheating

**Refrigerator:** Store for up to 1 day. Shrimp toughen when reheated.

**Reheating:** Warm gently in a covered pan over medium-low heat. Do not boil.

**Freezing:** Not recommended.
